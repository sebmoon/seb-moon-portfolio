## Overview

Our project focused on designing and manufacturing a bridge that would fit
seamlessly onto a jig and withstand a range of weights totalling 30 kg,
holding the largest amount of weight with the smallest deflection until
failure. After a series of tests and iterations, my group achieved the
smallest deflection while supporting the requested 30 kg weight.

## Research

Finding the right materials was one of the most significant challenges. We
weighed material weight, strength, and availability, and after researching
options and conducting tests settled on a combination of steel and wood —
a strong, sturdy structure with a clean appearance.

## Engineering Development

We considered truss, suspension, and arch bridge designs before settling on
a cantilever design that maximised strength while minimising deflection.

## Final Outcome

The bridge met all specifications and performed exceptionally well in
testing, achieving the smallest deflection in the cohort at the full 30 kg
load.
